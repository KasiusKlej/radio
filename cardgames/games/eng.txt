[GAMENAME]
Free cell
The object of a game is to put all the cards 
in upper 4 right places starting with ace of clubs,
ace of diamonds, ace of hearts and ace of spades
in each of 4 places.

[GAMENAME]
Four seasons
Put all the cards in corners starting with aces
in each corner. There are 5 cards in middle 5 positions
and other cards in the deck. Use middle positions to
store cards. You can move cards from deck to place
near deck or to 5 middle positions.

[GAMENAME]
Golf
The object of the game is to move all cards from the 
deck and from 7 columns to talon in ascending or descending 
sequence regardless of suit. 

[GAMENAME]
Alternate
Build 4 piles in alternate colours in ascending 
order on the aces and descending order on the kings.
Deal cards one at a time, building where possible or
discarding into any of four waste heaps. The top card 
of each heap is always available. When all the cards 
have been dealt, redeal without shufling. Two such redeals
are allowed.

[GAMENAME]
Canfield
  The goal of the game is to complete all four foundation piles
starting from aces and build upwards in suit sequence.
  Use tableau piles to store cards in descending sequence of 
alternating colour. There are four cards in tableau at begining 
of the game.
  Use top card from stock, where there are 13 cards at begining. 
  Other 34 cards are left in the deck. Take 3 cards from the deck
to your hand. Only top card is available. When all of 3 possibilities
were played, turn over and discard remamining of the 3 cards (that 
were not played) to talon. 
  When the deck is exsausted, the talon is turned over without being
shuffled and becomes the new deck, the cards in it again being turned 
over in packets of three. There is no limit to the number of times this 
can be done.

[GAMENAME]
Aud Lang Syne
Build upwards in suit from aces to kings.
Use tableau to store cards in descending
alternate order. Double click on stock to redeal.

[GAMENAME]
Castles in Spain
The object of a game is to complete all four
foundation columns buildig from aces ascending
to kings in suit. Use tableau to store cards
in descending alternate order. Double click the
face down card to turn it over.

[GAMENAME]
Klondike
(rank: very difficult)

Put all the cards in four foundation columns
starting from aces building in suit up to kings.
Use tableau to store cards in descending alternate
order. If vacancy in tableau is created during game,
you can put king or a sequence with a king as the base
card. Double click face down card to turn it over.
Once all possible moves in the tableau has been made,
turn the cards in the stock one by one, playing any 
cards that can be played onto a suitable card in the 
tableau or onto its foundation pile. Cards that cannot 
be played are put into the talon. One redeal of the talon
is allowed.

[GAMENAME]
Ship shape and Bristol fashion
The goal is to build 4 foundations upwards 
regardless of suit starting from aces. 
Packing within the tableau is downwards, 
ragardless of suit. Only one card at a time 
can be played. 
Double click stock to deal next three cards 
to reserve columns.

[GAMENAME]
The Battle of Ladies
Three Jacks are excluded from pack and are put far right, near stock.
Other Jack is at the top of pyramid. The goal is to build from 4 Queens
upwards to tens according to suit.
The name of the game originates from the old rule that the Queen 
whos' foundation is completed first claims the Jack and wins.
Cards at the side of pyramid are build downwards in alternate
colours. They can only be moved one by one. Spaces created are filled
by cards from the stock. When no more moves are possible deal 6
cards from stock to sides of pyramid by double clicking on stock.
When stock is exsausted, cards from sides of pyramid are taken and
redealed. Two such redeals are allowed. To redeal double click the
Jack card at the top of pyramid.

[GAMENAME]
Son of Fortune
The goal is to complete foundation columns from aces 
to kings building in suit.
Use two rows in tableau to store cards in descending 
suit matching order.
Cards that cannot be played from stock to foundation or tableau
are put to waste column (you can double click on stock to do so). 
Top card of the waste column is always available.
Any vacancies that are created in the tableau are filled
from waste pile or, if it's empty, from the stock.

[GAMENAME]
The Beleaguered Castle
(rank: very difficult)

The aim of the game is to complete 4 foundation piles
building from aces to kings. Use 8 side columns to 
store cards in descending alternate order.

[GAMENAME]
Bisley
(rank: very difficult)

Build downwards in suit starting with kings.
The tableau card can be moved to a card that 
matches in suit and is next to it in rank (eight 
of clubs could go to nine or seven of clubs).

[GAMENAME]
Fortress
(rank: very difficult)

Build four piles upwards in suit starting from aces.
The side cards can be moved to a card that 
matches in suit and is next to it in rank (eight 
of clubs could go to nine or seven of clubs).

[GAMENAME]
Grandpa's clock
There are 12 foundation columns forming a clock.
You must build up in suit on each, starting on 
foundation card and building until sequence reaches
the number of the hours at which the foundation card sits.
Use five tableau rows to store cards in descending order
regardless of suit. Double click the tableau card to turn 
it over.

[GAMENAME]
Princ Albert
Build upwards in suit starting from aces.
Use tableau (9 columns) to store cards in 
alternate descending order. 
Seven cards from the reserve can be played 
on foundation or tableau but not back to the 
reserve.

[GAMENAME]
Quadrille
You can play this game by double clicking on stock.

  (Cards are turned up from the stock one at a time.
The foundation cards are fives and sixes. Fives go
on north, west, east and south positions, sixes go
between them. On fives you can build down in suit 
to aces, on sixes you can build up to jacks. Queens
play no part in the game. When they turn up, they 
are put in the middle position.
Two redeals are allowed after the stock is exsausted.)

[GAMENAME]
Raglan
The goal is to build four foundations from aces
to kings.
Use tableau to store cards in alternate descending order.
Double click facedown card to turn it over.
The six cards from reserve can be put to foundation
but can not be put to tableau.
